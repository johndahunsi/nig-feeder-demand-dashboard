The ratio of the simultaneous feeder peak to the arithmetic sum of individual household peaks. Reflects demand diversity across thousands of connections. Paper central value: 0.75. Monte Carlo range: 0.50–0.90. Higher CF means less diversity — all households peak at the same time.

About this dashboard
Nigeria FES Demand Simulator · IEEE PowerAfrica 2026 · Dashboard v9 · Simulator v16
What this model does
This dashboard implements an original stochastic demand methodology for Nigerian 11 kV distribution feeders. It computes unsuppressed electricity demand — what a feeder will actually face the moment 24-hour reliable supply is established — rather than the metered consumption recorded under chronic rationing (national urban average: 6.6 h/day).

The framework introduces five original modelling layers: a six-profile BTM coping-asset taxonomy with year-by-year hardware retirement, a twelve-appliance supply-availability scaling model distinguishing continuous-use, task-completion, and supply-unlocked loads, a three-node sandcrete RC thermal model, a post-COVID SME/WFH occupancy layer, and a post-hoc price-elasticity correction. The Markov-chain switching engine and Nigerian appliance penetration data that underpin the appliance engine are acknowledged in the Sources section of this tab.
Key model concepts
BTM Migration — demand formerly served by diesel generators that becomes grid-visible on Day 1. Price-insensitive and meter-stable.
Supply scale S₀ = λ·(24/H₀) — how much appliance use increases when rationing ends. H₀ is the average daily duration of simultaneous whole-feeder energisation, measured at the feeder head and averaged over a habit-formation window (minimum 12 weeks, ideally covering a full seasonal cycle). This is not the DisCo's nominated band allocation, which may reflect sectionalized operation in which different laterals are energised at different times. Using a sectionalized average overstates the supply hours that household coping-asset portfolios were calibrated against, and therefore understates Day-1 grid draw. The Markov switching probabilities reflect the behavioural equilibrium households form around the simultaneous supply they actually experience. The NERC service bands (Band A ≥20 h → Band E 4 h) are useful anchors for sensitivity but are not the primary input. λ>1 captures new appliance acquisition unlocked by reliable supply, but diminishes above 14 h/day.
Supply-unlocked load — demand that didn't exist under rationing (electric cooking). Penetration grows through the concession as reliability is demonstrated.
Sandcrete nocturnal lag — hollow block walls store daytime solar heat and release it at night, keeping indoor T 8–10°C above outdoor T until 02:00.
WFH/SME plateau — 30–40% of upper-tier connections host home-based businesses, sustaining daytime demand at 60–80% of the evening peak.
Five model contributions · IEEE PowerAfrica 2026
i
Six-profile BTM household taxonomy
Multi-asset ownership modelled jointly. Year-by-year netting eligibility tracked as generators retire (all gone by Y8) and solar panels age. Panel power degradation (0.5%/yr, IEA PVPS 2023) applied per household — zero effect on post-sunset peak; shallows midday duck-curve progressively. BESS capacity fade ≈20% after 5yr SSA cycling (Uddin et al. 2017); 5–6yr household replacement cycle assumed (≥85% capacity at retirement). Model limitations (Y10–20): Solar replacement not modelled (Y20 penetration is a lower bound; MTF 5: ~20% model vs ~40–55% with replacement). TOU-arbitrage BESS omitted — Y15–20 BESS conservative for high-tariff scenarios. By Y15, >80% of the ring is pure-grid under baseline pathways. Both biases are upward — conservative for sizing.
ii
12-appliance supply-availability scaling
Four scaling classes: continuous-use (S₀), AC (0.8S₀), throughput-bounded (0.65S₀ for washing machine and electric cooker), and task-completion (1.0 for boiler, pump, iron, microwave). Six appliances extend the Richardson base six.
iii
Sandcrete three-node RC thermal model
Indoor air temperature decoupled from outdoor via wall thermal storage. Captures nocturnal cooling load that outdoor temperature alone misses.
iv
Post-COVID SME/WFH occupancy model
30–40% of upper-tier households host home-based businesses. Produces sustained daytime plateau absent from all pre-2020 models.
v
Post-hoc price-elasticity correction
Separates the price-inelastic BTM fraction from the elastic organic load analytically, without re-running the simulation.
Identified model extensions · out of scope in this round
EV charging (Zone A) — Logistic adoption curve, 0.05–0.09 MW at Year 5, 0.30–0.45 MW at Year 20. Extends evening peak. Excluded: penetration data requires Zone A household income survey.
Differential religious holiday modifiers — Current h_mod applies uniformly across all households. Self-employed Christians do not observe Islamic feasts. Ramadan Iftar spike unmodelled. Fix: add religion_share feeder parameter. Peak error on Eid days: ~15–20% overestimate.
Commercial/institutional consumers — Offices, plazas, banks, schools operate on C1/C2 tariff connections. Per-connection BTM migration from one commercial building equals 50–100 residential households. Aggregate omission: 0.3–0.8 MW. Fix: one-day field survey at franchise commencement.
Dishwasher — Identified but excluded from model. No Nigerian-specific penetration data. Estimated 5–12% Zone A. Stage 3 extension pending connection-level survey.

λ = 1: demand scales proportionally with supply hours only.
λ > 1: additional latent demand from reliability-driven appliance acquisition (households buy appliances they could not use under rationing). Capped at 1.3 for Band E feeders (H₀ < 8 h/day) based on observed unsuppressed-to-suppressed consumption ratios in the PeopleSuN Band E cohort. Implausible above 1.1 when H₀ > 14 h/day.
The fraction of generator-owning households that currently have working generators. At 100% all BTM profiles are fully active. Reducing this scales down the BTM migration component — modelling a feeder where generator retirement is already partially complete. Affects CO₂ displacement estimates and BTM decomposition.
Diurnal Controls
Controls the hourly profile shape on the Diurnal Profiles and Profile Explorer tabs. The 7.88 MW design-day peak is the worst-case hour on the hottest hot-dry day. Seasonal-average evening peaks are lower: 5.66 MW (hot-dry), 5.40 MW (Harmattan), 4.84 MW (wet).
Sets the planning year for the Diurnal Profiles and Profile Explorer tabs. Does not affect the 20-Year Trajectory tab, which shows the full concession horizon simultaneously.

Tariff–demand decoupling: The NERC Service Band determines the statutory tariff floor; actual supply hours H₀ (set via the Model Controls slider) determine unsuppressed demand via S₀. The commercially attractive franchise scenario — high-tier composition + low H₀ + Band C/D designation — maximises both demand uplift and revenue at a tariff households can afford relative to diesel. About this dashboard
Nigeria FES Demand Simulator · IEEE PowerAfrica 2026 · Dashboard v9 · Simulator v16
What this model does
This dashboard implements an original stochastic demand methodology for Nigerian 11 kV distribution feeders. It computes unsuppressed electricity demand — what a feeder will actually face the moment 24-hour reliable supply is established — rather than the metered consumption recorded under chronic rationing (national urban average: 6.6 h/day).

The framework introduces five original modelling layers: a six-profile BTM coping-asset taxonomy with year-by-year hardware retirement, a twelve-appliance supply-availability scaling model distinguishing continuous-use, task-completion, and supply-unlocked loads, a three-node sandcrete RC thermal model, a post-COVID SME/WFH occupancy layer, and a post-hoc price-elasticity correction. The Markov-chain switching engine and Nigerian appliance penetration data that underpin the appliance engine are acknowledged in the Sources section of this tab.
Key model concepts
BTM Migration — demand formerly served by diesel generators that becomes grid-visible on Day 1. Price-insensitive and meter-stable.
Supply scale S₀ = λ·(24/H₀) — how much appliance use increases when rationing ends. H₀ is the average daily duration of simultaneous whole-feeder energisation, measured at the feeder head and averaged over a habit-formation window (minimum 12 weeks, ideally covering a full seasonal cycle). This is not the DisCo's nominated band allocation, which may reflect sectionalized operation in which different laterals are energised at different times. Using a sectionalized average overstates the supply hours that household coping-asset portfolios were calibrated against, and therefore understates Day-1 grid draw. The Markov switching probabilities reflect the behavioural equilibrium households form around the simultaneous supply they actually experience. The NERC service bands (Band A ≥20 h → Band E 4 h) are useful anchors for sensitivity but are not the primary input. λ>1 captures new appliance acquisition unlocked by reliable supply, but diminishes above 14 h/day.
Supply-unlocked load — demand that didn't exist under rationing (electric cooking). Penetration grows through the concession as reliability is demonstrated.
Sandcrete nocturnal lag — hollow block walls store daytime solar heat and release it at night, keeping indoor T 8–10°C above outdoor T until 02:00.
WFH/SME plateau — 30–40% of upper-tier connections host home-based businesses, sustaining daytime demand at 60–80% of the evening peak.
Five model contributions · IEEE PowerAfrica 2026
i
Six-profile BTM household taxonomy
Multi-asset ownership modelled jointly. Year-by-year netting eligibility tracked as generators retire (all gone by Y8) and solar panels age. Panel power degradation (0.5%/yr, IEA PVPS 2023) applied per household — zero effect on post-sunset peak; shallows midday duck-curve progressively. BESS capacity fade ≈20% after 5yr SSA cycling (Uddin et al. 2017); 5–6yr household replacement cycle assumed (≥85% capacity at retirement). Model limitations (Y10–20): Solar replacement not modelled (Y20 penetration is a lower bound; MTF 5: ~20% model vs ~40–55% with replacement). TOU-arbitrage BESS omitted — Y15–20 BESS conservative for high-tariff scenarios. By Y15, >80% of the ring is pure-grid under baseline pathways. Both biases are upward — conservative for sizing.
ii
12-appliance supply-availability scaling
Four scaling classes: continuous-use (S₀), AC (0.8S₀), throughput-bounded (0.65S₀ for washing machine and electric cooker), and task-completion (1.0 for boiler, pump, iron, microwave). Six appliances extend the Richardson base six.
iii
Sandcrete three-node RC thermal model
Indoor air temperature decoupled from outdoor via wall thermal storage. Captures nocturnal cooling load that outdoor temperature alone misses.
iv
Post-COVID SME/WFH occupancy model
30–40% of upper-tier households host home-based businesses. Produces sustained daytime plateau absent from all pre-2020 models.
v
Post-hoc price-elasticity correction
Separates the price-inelastic BTM fraction from the elastic organic load analytically, without re-running the simulation.
Identified model extensions · out of scope in this round
EV charging (Zone A) — Logistic adoption curve, 0.05–0.09 MW at Year 5, 0.30–0.45 MW at Year 20. Extends evening peak. Excluded: penetration data requires Zone A household income survey.
Differential religious holiday modifiers — Current h_mod applies uniformly across all households. Self-employed Christians do not observe Islamic feasts. Ramadan Iftar spike unmodelled. Fix: add religion_share feeder parameter. Peak error on Eid days: ~15–20% overestimate.
Commercial/institutional consumers — Offices, plazas, banks, schools operate on C1/C2 tariff connections. Per-connection BTM migration from one commercial building equals 50–100 residential households. Aggregate omission: 0.3–0.8 MW. Fix: one-day field survey at franchise commencement.
Dishwasher — Identified but excluded from model. No Nigerian-specific penetration data. Estimated 5–12% Zone A. Stage 3 extension pending connection-level survey.
